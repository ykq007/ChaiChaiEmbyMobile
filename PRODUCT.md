# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

android

## Users

People who use a personal or shared Emby server and want a touch-first way to find and watch their media on Android phones, tablets, split-screen windows, and foldables. Their primary task is completing the First Viewing Loop without losing context as the available window or device posture changes.

## Product Purpose

The Mobile Client makes an Emby library feel coherent and dependable on touch-first Android devices. Success means a user can add one server, discover a movie or episode, inspect it, play it with the right tracks, leave, and reliably resume from server-synced progress through an Adaptive Layout.

## Positioning

A quiet personal screening room for Emby that combines artwork-led discovery with dependable Android-native interaction across window sizes.

## Brand Personality

Cinematic, calm, and assured. The experience should borrow media density and artwork-led browsing from Plex, restraint and typographic focus from Apple TV, and familiar playback conventions from YouTube without imitating any one product.

## Anti-references

Avoid oversized promotional rows that subordinate the user's library, visually cluttered media dashboards, and generic Material 3 surfaces dominated by decorative colored cards.

## Design Principles

- Keep the user's media—not the application chrome—as the visual protagonist.
- Preserve journey, selection, scroll, and playback context across every Adaptive Layout transition.
- Use familiar Android interaction patterns so the interface disappears into watching.
- Increase useful density and simultaneous context as space grows; never merely stretch the compact layout.
- Make loading, partial failure, emptiness, and recovery understandable without breaking the viewing journey.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Meet WCAG AA as the baseline. Support large system text, TalkBack semantics and traversal, sufficient contrast without relying on color alone, reduced or removed motion, and touch targets of at least 48dp with adequate separation.
